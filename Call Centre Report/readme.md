
The primary objective of this project was to transform raw customer service and sales call data into a strategic, easy-to-read asset for management and operational leaders.
## Key Features & Visualizations
### High-Level KPI Cards: 
Instantly displays overarching metrics, including total calls, revenue/amount, cumulative call duration, average customer rating, and a count of "happy callers."
### Time-Series Analysis:
Includes a month-over-month line chart to track seasonal call trends, paired with a horizontal bar chart breaking down call volume by day of the week.
### Geographic Demographic Breakdown:
Utilizes a stacked column chart to compare caller gender ratios across three major hubs: Cincinnati, Cleveland, and Columbus.
### Customer Satisfaction Distribution:
A histogram mapping the frequency of customer ratings on a 1-to-5 scale.
### Dynamic Agent Drill-Down:
Features an interactive slicer for representatives (R01–R05). Selecting a specific representative dynamically updates the dashboard to show their individual metrics, their percentage of total calls handled, and their overall performance rank.
### Granular Revenue Matrix:
A detailed table cross-tabulating revenue by specific client IDs, operational region, and the assigned representative.


# Business Impact
This project demonstrates the ability to synthesize complex, multi-layered datasets into an intuitive interface. By surfacing trends in both customer satisfaction and individual agent performance, this dashboard empowers stakeholders to quickly identify bottlenecks, optimize resource allocation, and drive continuous operational improvement.
